Global Certification Comparison: PSE / ETL / KC / CE for Food Waste Disposers

By Shihao Intelligent Technical Team June 5, 2026 10 min read Certifications ┬╖ Compliance

If you plan to sell an electric food waste disposer internationally, the single most critical pre-launch checklist item is regulatory compliance. Each major market has its own mandatory safety certification scheme тА?and selling without the correct mark is not just a business risk, it's a legal one.

This guide covers the four certification frameworks most relevant to B2B buyers of electric kitchen composters: PSE (Japan), ETL/UL (North America), KC (Korea), and CE + ERP (Europe). For each, we explain what the mark means, what testing it requires, how long it takes, and how OEM manufacturing can eliminate the burden entirely.

Japan: PSE Certification

What Is PSE?

PSE (Product Safety Electrical Appliance and Material) is Japan's mandatory safety certification, administered under the Electrical Appliance and Material Safety Act (DENKIANZEN). Electric food waste disposers are classified as specified electrical appliances (чЙ╣хоЪщЫ╗ц░ЧчФихУБ), requiring third-party testing by a designated conformity assessment body.

Products without the PSE diamond mark (тЧ? cannot be legally sold, imported, or distributed in Japan. Customs will detain non-compliant shipments.

What Does PSE Testing Cover?

  • Insulation resistance and dielectric strength
  • Temperature rise under load
  • Overload and abnormal operation tests
  • Mechanical strength and stability
  • Motor protection (thermal cutout performance)
  • Ground continuity testing

North America: ETL / UL Certification

What Is ETL?

ETL (formerly Electrical Testing Laboratories) is a Nationally Recognized Testing Laboratory (NRTL) mark that evaluates products to UL (Underwriters Laboratories) standards. Both ETL and UL marks are accepted by US retailers, code authorities, and enforcement agencies. Neither is technically legally mandated by federal law, but most major US retailers (Target, Home Depot, Amazon) require one as a condition of sale.

In Canada, the CSA mark (or equivalent NRTLs listing to Canadian standards) is required for retail. Shihao's ETL-certified models can be extended to cover Canadian markets through cETL listing.

Relevant Standards

  • UL 430 тА?Household waste disposers
  • UL 60335-1 тА?Safety of household appliances (general)
  • UL 60335-2-16 тА?Particular requirements for food waste disposers
  • FCC Part 15 тА?If product includes any wireless/Bluetooth functionality

Timeline and Cost

ETL testing from scratch typically takes 2тА? months, including sample submission, lab testing, and listing application. Costs range from $5,000тА?15,000 USD depending on the lab and whether the product requires engineering changes to pass.

Voltage Note

North American voltage is 110тА?20V at 60Hz тА?significantly different from European 220тА?40V at 50Hz. Products must be factory-configured for 110V or include a universal voltage motor. Shihao's 500W (100V) / 550W (110тА?30V) architecture supports this natively on all series.

Korea: KC Certification

What Is KC?

KC (Korea Certification) is South Korea's mandatory safety mark, governed by the Electrical Appliances and Consumer Products Safety Control Act, administered by KATS (Korean Agency for Technology and Standards). Electric food waste disposers are designated products requiring mandatory KC registration and factory audit.

Korea is Shihao's largest single OEM market, and almost our entire product catalog is KC-certified. The KC mark covers safety, EMC (electromagnetic compatibility), and energy efficiency requirements.

What KC Testing Covers

  • Safety testing to K 60335-1 and K 60335-2-16 standards
  • EMC testing (KN 61000 series) тА?radiated and conducted emissions
  • Factory audit by KATS or recognized body (required annually)
  • Product registration in the KC information system

Timeline and Cost

KC certification from scratch takes 3тА? months, including factory audit scheduling (often the longest bottleneck). Cost: approximately $10,000тА?25,000 USD, excluding factory preparation costs. Korean language documentation is required throughout.

Key Operational Requirement

KC-certified products must display the KC mark on both the product and packaging, with the certificate number. Korean-language user manuals are mandatory. Shihao provides all of these as part of the OEM package for Korean-market models.

Europe: CE + ERP

CE Marking

CE marking is mandatory for all electrical products sold in the European Economic Area (EEA). Unlike PSE or KC, CE is a self-declaration тА?the manufacturer or importer declares conformity with applicable EU Directives and is responsible for the technical file. However, in practice, third-party lab testing is strongly recommended and often contractually required by European distributors.

Relevant EU Directives for food waste disposers:

  • LVD (Low Voltage Directive 2014/35/EU) тА?electrical safety
  • EMC Directive (2014/30/EU) тА?electromagnetic compatibility
  • RoHS Directive (2011/65/EU) тА?restriction of hazardous substances
  • WEEE Directive (2012/19/EU) тА?take-back and recycling obligations

ERP (Energy-Related Products Regulation)

The EU's ERP Regulation now applies to food waste disposers sold in Europe, requiring:

  • Energy efficiency measurement and documentation
  • Standby and off-mode power limits
  • Product information sheet (PIS) published and accessible
  • EPREL registration (EU product registration database)

Shihao holds ERP compliance documentation across qualifying models, with EPREL registration available as part of the EU OEM package.

Timeline

CE + ERP documentation from scratch takes 2тА? months, primarily driven by EMC lab testing and technical file compilation. Cost varies significantly тА?from $3,000 (self-declaration with existing test data) to $15,000+ (full third-party testing for all directives). Shihao provides Declaration of Conformity (DoC) and full technical files for EU-bound OEM shipments.

Maintaining Certification: What Happens After Launch

Certifications are not a one-time event. Several ongoing obligations apply:

  • PSE: Annual factory audit. Any product specification change requires re-testing and re-certification before sale.
  • KC: Annual factory audit by KATS. Significant product changes trigger re-registration.
  • ETL: Follow-up inspections (FUI) by Intertek on a periodic basis. Production must remain consistent with the tested configuration.
  • CE: Technical file must be maintained and updated with any design change. ERP EPREL registration must be kept current.

As an OEM partner, Shihao manages all of these obligations as part of our ongoing manufacturing relationship. You receive updated certificates and documentation automatically.
