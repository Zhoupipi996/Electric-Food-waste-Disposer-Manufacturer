What to Test ‚Ä?A Buyer's Evaluation Checklist

Most first-time buyers focus on aesthetics and basic function. That's necessary, but not sufficient. Here is a structured evaluation framework covering all the dimensions that matter for a commercial launch:

1. Unboxing and First Impression

  • Does the packaging arrive undamaged? (Assess courier-readiness)
  • Is the branding applied cleanly ‚Ä?no bleed, no misalignment?
  • Is the user manual present, in the correct language, and accurate?
  • Are all accessories included? (Power cord, filter, user guide, warranty card)
  • Does the unit feel premium at first touch ‚Ä?weight, surface finish, lid action?

2. Basic Operation Test

  • Power on cleanly with no error codes on first use?
  • Touch pad controls responsive and clearly labelled?
  • LED display (where applicable) legible in normal room lighting?
  • Lid opens and seals correctly ‚Ä?no rattling or gap?
  • Waste bucket removes and reinserts smoothly?

3. Processing Performance Test

This is the most important evaluation stage. Run at least three processing cycles with different waste types:

  1. Cycle 1 ‚Ä?Standard Mixed Waste (half capacity)

    Load with fruit peels, vegetable scraps, eggshells, and bread. Fill to approximately 50% of bucket capacity. Run a full cycle. Measure: cycle time, noise level at 1 metre, output texture (should be dry, crumbly powder/granules), and residual odour after lid opens.

  2. Cycle 2 ‚Ä?Protein-Heavy Waste (half capacity)

    Load with cooked chicken scraps, fish trimmings, and meat fat. This is the most odour-intensive test. Assess: cycle time extension vs cycle 1, odour during operation (should be minimal outside the unit), output texture, and whether the activated carbon filter handles the load.

  3. Cycle 3 ‚Ä?Full Capacity Load

    Load to maximum capacity with a mix of wet and dry waste. Measure: total cycle time (should be 4‚Ä? hours for 3L‚Ä?L units), noise consistency throughout, volume reduction achieved (target: ‚Č?0% reduction), and output moisture (should be dry enough to crumble by hand).

4. Acoustic Test

Use a smartphone sound meter app (free tools are sufficient for this evaluation) to measure:

  • Operating noise at 1 metre during active grinding phase (target: ‚Č?5 dB for standard models, ‚Č?5 dB for 5.5L flagship)
  • Noise at 3 metres (relevant for open-plan kitchen placements)
  • Vibration transmitted to countertop (place hand flat on counter surface)
  • Noise at 1am vs 1pm ‚Ä?subjective perception testing in a quiet environment

5. Odour and Air Quality Test

  • Open the lid immediately after cycle completion ‚Ä?any noticeable residual odour?
  • Sniff the air vent during operation ‚Ä?activated carbon should suppress all food odour
  • Test in a small enclosed kitchen (worst-case environment) ‚Ä?does odour escape?

6. Cleaning and Maintenance Test

  • Remove the waste bucket ‚Ä?is it dishwasher-safe? Does food residue release easily?
  • Detach and reassemble the air duct system (where applicable) ‚Ä?is it intuitive?
  • Clean the grinding chamber ‚Ä?accessible without tools?
  • Inspect the activated carbon filter ‚Ä?easy to locate and replace?

7. Brand Compliance Review

  • Logo position, size, and colour match your brand guidelines?
  • Product colour matches your approved Pantone/RAL reference?
  • Packaging artwork matches your approved design file?
  • Certification marks visible and correctly positioned?
  • Barcode / UPC scannable?

How to Submit Effective Feedback

Clear, specific feedback is the difference between a one-revision process and a four-revision process. Shihao provides a structured Sample Evaluation Report (SER) template with every sample shipment. We recommend completing it fully before submitting feedback.

For each issue identified, specify:

  • Category: Is this a branding issue, a performance issue, a packaging issue, or a documentation issue?
  • Severity: Blocker (cannot proceed), Major (must fix before mass production), Minor (nice-to-fix, acceptable if unchanged)
  • Reference: Photo, video, or timestamp where possible
  • Expected standard: What should it look like / sound like / feel like?

ūüďĻ Video Evidence Is Invaluable

If you identify a noise issue, a UI quirk, or a performance problem, a short video (even filmed on a smartphone) eliminates ambiguity immediately. Our engineering team can diagnose and confirm a fix far faster from a 30-second video than from a written description.

Sample Approval and Moving to Mass Production

Once you're satisfied with the sample, the approval process is straightforward:

  1. Submit Signed Sample Approval Form

    Shihao provides a one-page Sample Approval Confirmation (SAC) form. This documents the exact product specifications ‚Ä?model, color, logo position, firmware version, accessories, and packaging ‚Ä?that are to be replicated in mass production. Both parties sign it.

  2. Issue Purchase Order (PO)

    Your formal PO referencing the approved sample specifications. Include: model code, quantity, delivery destination, required ship date, and any specific QC requirements (e.g. third-party pre-shipment inspection).

  3. Deposit Payment

    Standard payment terms are 30% deposit on PO, 70% balance before shipment. For established partners, extended terms are available on request. Sample fee is deducted from the deposit invoice.

  4. Production Confirmation

    Shihao confirms production start date, estimated completion date, and shipment window. Weekly production updates are provided for orders of 1,000+ units.

  5. Pre-Shipment Inspection (PSI)

    For orders of 500+ units, Shihao conducts an internal pre-shipment inspection against the approved sample specification. Results (including pass/fail data, photos, and measurement records) are provided before goods are shipped. Partners may also arrange third-party PSI (SGS, Intertek, BV) at their own cost ‚Ä?Shihao facilitates access.
